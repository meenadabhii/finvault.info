Essential Investment Approaches

Growth Investing

Growth investing focuses on capital appreciation by investing in companies expected to grow at an above-average rate compared to their industry or the overall market.

Key Characteristics:

  • Targets companies with strong earnings growth
  • Often invests in technology and innovative sectors
  • Higher risk with potential for higher returns
  • Less focus on dividend payments

Best For:

Investors with longer time horizons (10+ years) who can tolerate market volatility and want to build substantial wealth over time.

Value Investing

Value investing involves identifying undervalued stocks that trade for less than their intrinsic values, often determined through fundamental analysis.

Key Characteristics:

  • Seeks stocks trading below their "true" value
  • Often focuses on companies with strong fundamentals
  • May involve dividend-paying stocks
  • Requires patience as undervaluation may persist

Best For:

Conservative investors looking for margin of safety and willing to hold investments for medium to long term (5+ years).

Index Investing

Index investing involves buying index funds or ETFs that track a market index, providing broad market exposure with low operating expenses.

Key Characteristics:

  • Passive investment approach
  • Lower fees than actively managed funds
  • Diversification across many securities
  • Performance matches the market (no outperformance)

Best For:

Investors seeking market-average returns with minimal effort, ideal for retirement accounts and long-term wealth building.

Dividend Investing

Dividend investing focuses on stocks that pay regular dividends, providing income along with potential capital appreciation.

Key Characteristics:

  • Focus on companies with consistent dividend payments
  • Potential for growing dividend income over time
  • Often involves mature, stable companies
  • Dividends can be reinvested for compounding

Best For:

Income-focused investors, retirees, or those seeking lower volatility while still participating in market growth.

Asset Allocation Guide

A balanced portfolio typically includes a mix of these asset classes:

Stocks 50-70%
Bonds 20-40%
Real Estate 5-15%

* Percentages vary based on age, risk tolerance, and goals.

Key Investment Terms

ROI (Return on Investment)

A measure of the profitability of an investment, calculated as (Gain - Cost) / Cost.

Diversification

Spreading investments across various assets to reduce risk.

Market Capitalization

Total market value of a company's outstanding shares.

P/E Ratio

Price-to-Earnings ratio, comparing a company's share price to its earnings.
