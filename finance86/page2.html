Wealth Building Techniques

Diversification Strategy

Allocate across stocks (60%), bonds (30%), and alternatives (10%). Rebalance quarterly to maintain ratios. International exposure should be 20-40% of equity holdings.

Compound Interest Power

$500/month at 7% annual return becomes $1.1M in 40 years. Starting 10 years earlier could yield $2.3M. Tax-advantaged accounts amplify this effect dramatically.

Risk Management Framework

Your stock allocation should be (110 - your age). Use stop-loss orders (5-10% below purchase) and position sizing (1-2% of portfolio per trade) to limit downside.

Dollar-Cost Averaging

Invest fixed amounts regularly (e.g., $500/month) regardless of market conditions. Reduces emotional investing and lowers average share cost over time.

Sector Rotation Strategy

Value vs Growth Investing

Value stocks (low P/E) outperform in rising rate environments, while growth stocks (high earnings growth) excel during low-rate periods. Blend both for balance.

Tax-Efficient Investing

Place high-dividend assets in retirement accounts and growth stocks in taxable accounts. Harvest tax losses annually to offset gains. Hold assets >1 year for lower capital gains rates.
